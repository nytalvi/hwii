The Ritual

People create everyday. I've heard that, specifically in Judaism, the Sabbath is given to us as a break from creating, just as God took a break from creating. I don't create worlds, but I do create writings, songs, drawings, memories...

I've started writing a lot more recently, but it poses the question: for whom?

I can't depend on anyone to read my things, and I don't have to. Writing is like a silent ritual, between myself and the divine, and infinitely meaningful no matter how many eyes it reaches.[1]

It is personally satisfying to me to complete a paper and publish it on my website, knowing that I made something real. So that's why I enjoy writing, and will continue to do so as long as I can.

References & Notes

  1. In Ancient Egypt, priests would do offerings where they'd present food, incense, etc. to their deities, and many would do it as a daily ritual. The priests would then eat them, which symbolized a communion between the divine and mortal. In Hinduism, the practice of puja is similar, where devotees would offer food, flowers, and prayers to their deities. These rituals were often performed at home.
